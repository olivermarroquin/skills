# Synthesis guide — talking points + engagement plan

How to turn the three `analysis/` files (brief, homepage audit, competitor glance) into the three
call materials. Keep everything plain-language and specific. The test: every claim on the call
sheet should trace to a line in one of the analysis files.

## talking-points.md

One screen. The operator glances at this mid-call.

- **30-second read.** Who they are (1 line from brief §1), what's working (1 genuine strength — open
  warm), the biggest fixable problem (the #1 item from the audit's priority-fix list), and what it's
  costing them (tie to leads/jobs — use the competitor gap).
- **Three things to point at.** Pull two from the homepage audit's priority-fix list (concrete:
  "no schema markup, so Google can't read your services"; "copyright still says 2023") and one from
  the competitor glance ("AJ Long has 40+ city pages, you have 5"). Specific beats generic — never
  invent a defect that isn't in the audit.
- **Discovery questions.** 3 questions that (a) confirm a brief gap flagged `<confirm on call>`,
  (b) surface budget/urgency, (c) surface who owns their accounts (the ownership gate matters at
  onboarding). Phrase as curiosity, not interrogation.
- **One-sentence pitch.** The engagement framed as their outcome, not our deliverables.
- **Objection prep.** "Too expensive" → tie to cost of inaction. "I already have a guy" → point at
  the defects the current guy left. "Does SEO work?" → the competitor who's winning is the proof.

## engagement-plan.md

What we'd do if they sign — outcomes, not task lists. Mirror the EV/S&H phasing:

- **Phase 0 onboarding** (week 1): access handoff, baseline capture, GBP optimization.
- **Phase 1 foundation** (weeks 2–5): core service + city pages, schema, internal links.
- **Phase 2 authority** (weeks 6–10): backlinks, citations, reviews engine.
- **Phase 3 convert + iterate** (ongoing): conversion polish, reporting, monthly cadence.

State the headline outcome as a measurable result ("rank top-3 for your top services across N
cities in 90 days, and convert that into booked jobs"). List what we need from them (access +
content). Close with 2–3 lines on why us vs. their status quo or a generic agency: custom-coded
pages they own, data-driven, local-trade specialists.

## proposal/draft-pricing.md

See `pricing-anchors.md`. Propose numbers, mark them clearly as a ballpark, leave for the operator
to finalize. Anchor the price to the cost of inaction from the talking points.

## Honesty rules

- If the site is actually good, say so — the pitch becomes "amplify," not "rescue."
- If they look like a poor fit (out of area, no budget signal, dead business), flag it at the top of
  talking-points so the operator can qualify out fast.
- Never fabricate review counts, ranking positions, or defects. "Unknown — confirm on call" is a
  valid and useful entry.
