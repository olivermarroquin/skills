# Invoice email template

Standard email body for sending a Keelworks LLC invoice. Reference this file when sending; don't paste from memory — small wording drifts add up.

## Subject line

```
Invoice [NUMBER] — Keelworks [ENGAGEMENT TYPE]
```

Examples:
- `Invoice EVE-001 — Keelworks SEO onboarding`
- `Invoice SHC-001 — Keelworks SEO onboarding`
- `Invoice EVE-002 — Keelworks monthly retainer`

## Body

```
Hi [CLIENT NAME],

[PERSONAL OPENING LINE]

Attached is the invoice for the work we agreed to. [PAYMENT TIMING NOTE — flexible if applicable].

If anything looks off or you have questions, hit reply.

Thanks,
Oliver
```

## Notes on the variables

- **[CLIENT NAME]** — first name only (Ahmad, Mohammad). Matches how Oliver speaks to the client in conversation.
- **[PERSONAL OPENING LINE]** — short, grounded, references the actual conversation. Avoid generic filler.
  - Good: "Great talking with you yesterday."
  - Good: "Glad we got the kickoff done — appreciate the time."
  - Good: "Thanks again for the chat at the meeting."
  - Bad: "I hope this email finds you well." (filler)
  - Bad: "Please find attached..." (corporate)
- **[PAYMENT TIMING NOTE]** — only include if there's flexibility worth communicating. Examples:
  - "Pay whenever convenient before May 20" (when the due date is flexible)
  - "$250 around May 14 and $250 around May 16 — dates are flexible to match your cash flow" (for split-payment)
  - Omit entirely if the due date is firm.

## What NOT to include

- Don't restate the scope of work in the email body — that's what the PDF is for. The email is a delivery note, not a duplicate invoice.
- Don't apologize for the invoice ("sorry to bother you," "hope this isn't too much"). The work was agreed to; the invoice is professional follow-through.
- Don't add a "let me know if you'd like to pay another way" line unless you actually want to accept other methods. The PDF specifies Zelle; let it stand.
